Why Choosing the Right E-Commerce Model Matters

Starting an E-Commerce business is not just about selling products online. The choice of business model directly impacts revenue structure, cost management, scalability, and long-term sustainability. Many startups fail because they select a business model without analyzing:

  • Budget constraints
  • Risk tolerance
  • Target market
  • Technical capabilities
  • Growth expectations

Explore business model

Subscription Model

Customers pay recurring fees at regular intervals to access products or services continuously.


Examples: Netflix,Spotify,Amazon Prime,Microsoft 365

Affiliate Marketing

Individuals or businesses promote others products online and earn commission for every sale generated through referral links.


Examples: Popular platforms: Amazon Associates ,ClickBank,CJ Affiliate,ShareASale

Business to Customer

Businesses sell products or services directly to individual consumers through online or offline channels. Popular platforms:


Examples: Amazon,Flipkart,Nike (official website),Myntra

Marketplace Model

A marketplace business model connects buyers and sellers on a digital platform, facilitating transactions and earning commissions.


Examples: Amazon, Flipkart, eBay, Airbnb, Uber

Dorpshipping

Dropshipping is an e-commerce model where an online store sells products without keeping stock. When customers order, the store sends the order to a supplier, who packs and ships the product directly to the customer.


Example:Shopify,oberlo,spocket,WooCommerce

Business to Business

Businesses sell products or services directly to other businesses for resale, production, or operations. Popular platforms:


Examples: Alibaba,IndiaMART,Udaan,Salesforce
